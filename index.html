Satisfyer Pro 2

Reliable. Effective. About as discreet as a blender.

The Satisfyer Pro 2 is the dependable mid-range workhorse: proven air-pulse technology, 11 intensity levels, and a ~$40 price point. Three of our five testers said it "gets the job done" — honest praise, if not exactly poetry.

But it's loud. Noticeably louder than every other device we tested — one tester compared it to an electric toothbrush. If you care about noise, design, or discretion, the top two outclass it.

#4

The Rose

8.5 / 10 ~$30

Everyone has one. That doesn't mean it's the best one.

No air-pulse toy has captured the internet quite like The Rose. Hundreds of millions of TikTok views, a design cute enough to leave on your nightstand, and a ~$30 price tag that made it the gateway product for a generation of first-time buyers. We get the appeal.

But our testers found the hype outpaced the experience. The decorative petal crevices that make it look so pretty are a hygiene nightmare — two testers struggled to clean between them thoroughly. The suction is decent on higher settings but lacks the nuanced range of mid-tier competitors, and the motor rattles at higher intensities. A solid introduction to the category, but most testers outgrew it within weeks.

#5

Tracy's Dog OG Pro 2

7.8 / 10 ~$45

Sounds great on paper. Felt like a panic attack.

The Tracy's Dog OG Pro 2 promises to do it all with its dual-stimulation design: 10 suction patterns, 10 vibration settings, and a curved internal vibrating arm at ~$45. It's part air-pulse toy, part vibrator — and that's both its selling point and its downfall. Our testers found the 100+ pattern combinations overwhelming rather than enjoyable. Two described the sensation as "frantic," and the controls required constant fiddling that pulled them out of the moment.

The dual-stimulation design also makes the device significantly bulkier than a dedicated air-pulse toy — harder to position, less intuitive, and the vibrating motor adds noticeable noise. A jack-of-all-trades that masters none.
